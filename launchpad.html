About Young Pioneers Programme

The Young Pioneers Launchpad is the capstone of the Young Pioneers Programme, which is a 3-year curriculum designed by Get Set Learn in partnership with Cambridge University Press & Assessment, aligned with NEP 2020 and NCF 2023.

Launchpad Agenda

Day 1
Day 1
The first day of the Launchpad will set the stage for 39 shortlisted student teams to present their business ideas.
  • Format: On-ground in Mumbai with hybrid access for virtual attendees.
  • Time per Team: 7 mins Pitch + 3 mins Q&A.
  • Pitch Focus: Problem Understanding, Creativity, Teamwork, Communication & Presentation, Handling Jury Questions.
Day 2
Day 2
The top 10 teams shortlisted from Day 1 will pitch for the final time in front of the jurors to become the Top 5 winners.
  • Format:On-ground in Mumbai | Finale Day.
  • Teams: Top 10 shortlisted from Day 1.
  • Time per Team: 3 mins Storytelling Pitch + 2 mins Showcase + 3 mins Q&A.
  • Pitch Focus: Problem awareness, creativity in presentation, clarity, and story-led structure and delivery, Visuals & Branding, Team Collaboration and Communication, Handling Jury Questions.

Vedang Patel
Vedang completed his engineering in 2010 from Sardar Patel College of Engineering. After graduating, he worked as a financial analyst for two years. He had cleared CAT and got admission calls from three premier institutes in the country.

Right after the admission, he realised that the world of finance had stopped giving him joy and the intent to break monotony brought him to entrepreneurship.
Swathy Rohit
Swathy Rohit is the Founder CEO of Health Basix, which is devoted to the well-being of children through holistic physical and mental health care. With a Bachelor of Engineering (BE) in Mechanical Engineering from University of Virginia under her belt, Ms. Swathy is also skilled in operations, business planning, market research, manufacturing, and management.
Sharat Potharaju
Sharat is responsible for overall strategy and execution at Uniqode, which is on a mission to build an Operating System for businesses in the physical world. Sharat is also passionate about politics and public governance and has worked with Bangalore-based civic engagement non-profit Janaagraha in 2009. While there, he contributed to the award-winning Jaago Re Campaign, India's largest urban grassroots movement for voter registration.

He has a Master’s in Engineering Management from Duke University and a Bachelor’s in Chemical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras.
Jyoti Bharadwaj
Jyoti, founder of TeaFit, created India's first unsweetened beverage brand from her kitchen. Stemming from Jyoti’s deep-rooted belief in the power of natural ingredients, TeaFit offers functional beverages inspired by Ayurveda. Combining traditional wisdom with modern wellness trends, TeaFit’s formulations are designed to support balance and vitality. Jyoti’s commitment to sustainability, clean ingredients, and holistic well-being is at the heart of TeaFit, making it a go-to brand for health-conscious consumers looking for both flavor and function in their daily routine.
Vishal Kumar
Vishal Kumar is the CEO of Waste Warriors, a non-profit organization dedicated to safeguarding the pristine Indian Himalayas from solid waste. Under his leadership, Waste Warriors has earned Waste Warriors international recognition, including the prestigious Keeling Curve Prize, cementing its position as a pioneer in climate change mitigation. Vishal has a Bachelor’s in Material Science from the Indian Institute of Technology, Varanasi (formerly Banaras Hindu University).
Dr. Sheenu Jain
Dr. Sheenu Jain is a distinguished academic and entrepreneur. Her expertise includes curriculum design, managing startups, setting up entrepreneurship and incubation centers, establishing brand stores, and creating E-cells. She was selected by Goldman Sachs & London Business School for training 10,000 women entrepreneurs in India.

She has a PhD in Green Marketing and was recognized as a Young Management Scholar by IIM Calcutta. She also has a MCom, and MSc (Applied Psychology), along with certifications in Entrepreneurship education, mentoring, and training from ISB Hyderabad and the National Entrepreneurship Network.
Bharat Singhal
Bharat Singhal, Founder & CEO of Bili Hu Coffees, is an entrepreneur who has transformed Indian estate coffees into a luxury experience. From his MIT Pune design project to founding Bili Hu in 2017, he partners with South Indian growers to deliver premium coffee, served at iconic hotels like the Taj and Oberoi. Favored by figures like Ratan Tata, his brand embodies sophistication. Bharat's journey, celebrated at IIM Ahmedabad, showcases his dedication to excellence and innovation.
